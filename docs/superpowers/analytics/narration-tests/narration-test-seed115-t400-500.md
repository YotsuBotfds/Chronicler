## Prologue — The First 400 Turns

Four civilizations founded Aetheris. The Kethani Empire, Dorrathi Clans, and Ashkari Nomads fell within the first 76 turns — to war, famine, and twilight. The Selurian Republic, last of the founders, collapsed at turn 189 under internal pressure after reaching the Information Era three times and losing it twice.

What followed was an era of fragmentation. Northern Sapphire Coast — born from the Kethani collapse at turn 50 — became the dominant successor state before splintering into Lower Northern Sapphire Coast (Mistwood), Western Northern Sapphire Coast (Stormbreak Cliffs + Sapphire Coast), and Greater Western Northern Sapphire Coast (Thornwood). Northern Sapphire Coast was absorbed at turn 300, briefly restored at turn 302 with a military-dominant government (the only non-merchant government since the founding era), then absorbed again at turn 328.

The three fragments fought the same wars that their predecessors had: attacks that failed, hostages that changed nothing, federations that collapsed before the ink dried. Knowledge drained from the world — tech regressions hit every state. Lower NSC fell to the Renaissance Era. Western NSC lost and regained the Information Era. Greater Western NSC lost it to Industrial.

At turn 398, the Congress of Mistwood achieved peace — the first successful congress in Aetherian history. It lasted one turn before Greater Western NSC attacked again.

At turn 400: Lower NSC (Mistwood, pop 11, treasury 1914, renaissance era, merchant 71%), Western NSC (Stormbreak Cliffs + Sapphire Coast, pop 4, treasury 534, information era, merchant 55%), Greater Western NSC (Thornwood, pop 10, treasury 1552, information era, cultural 44%).

### Key Narrative Arcs

1. **The Technological Collapse (turns 400-414):** An industrial disaster poisons Thornwood (turn 404) — the first tech_accident event in the simulation. Tech regressions cascade: Lower NSC falls to medieval, Greater Western NSC loses information era, Western NSC loses information era. By turn 414, no state in Aetheris holds the Information Era. The world that had reached the pinnacle of knowledge three hundred turns ago is now governed by medieval and industrial-era states.

2. **The Last Absorption (turns 428-448):** Greater Western NSC enters twilight at turn 428 and is absorbed at turn 448. A second Congress of Mistwood achieves peace at turn 431. After Greater Western NSC's absorption, only two states remain for the final time: Lower NSC (Mistwood + Thornwood) and Western NSC (Stormbreak Cliffs + Sapphire Coast).

3. **The Final Equilibrium (turns 450-499):** Two states, neither able to challenge the other, neither able to recover the knowledge their ancestors possessed. Lower NSC is wealthy (treasury ~1600), medieval era, merchant-dominated. Western NSC is poor (treasury near zero, eventually negative), industrial era, cultural-dominant. Populations slowly recover — Lower NSC reaches 119 thousand at turn 480, the highest population since the founding era. Western NSC reaches 63 thousand. Amber Valley oscillates between forest and plains (turn 468). The world ends not with conquest or collapse but with exhaustion.

4. **Western NSC's final regression (turn 494):** In the simulation's closing turns, Western NSC loses the Industrial Era, falling to Renaissance. The technological arc of Aetheris ends with every surviving state below the era the Selurian Republic entered the world with.
