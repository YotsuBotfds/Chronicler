## Prologue — The First 300 Turns

Four civilizations founded Aetheris. All four are gone. The Kethani Empire fell at turn 50, the Dorrathi Clans at turn 49, the Ashkari Nomads at turn 76, and the Selurian Republic — the last founder — collapsed under internal pressure at turn 189.

What survived were successor states. Northern Sapphire Coast, born from the Kethani Empire's collapse, became the dominant power of the middle period before fragmenting into three pieces at turns 185 and 195: the parent Northern Sapphire Coast (Sapphire Coast + Stormbreak Cliffs), Lower Northern Sapphire Coast (Mistwood), and Western Northern Sapphire Coast (Thornwood). All three were Information Era merchant states with massive treasuries and tiny populations — six to thirty thousand citizens governing one region apiece.

The wars of the successor states (turns 263-300) saw Western NSC seize Stormbreak Cliffs in the only successful conquest in a century, followed by decades of futile warfare where every attacker lost. Northern Sapphire Coast entered twilight at turn 280 and was absorbed at turn 300. The land itself changed: Mistwood and Thornwood lost their forests, though Amber Valley regenerated its own at turn 289.

At turn 300, two states remained: Lower Northern Sapphire Coast (Mistwood, pop 3, treasury 2303, industrial era) and Western Northern Sapphire Coast (Thornwood + Stormbreak Cliffs + Sapphire Coast, pop 6, treasury 2636, information era). Nine thousand people on a continent that had sustained over two hundred thousand.

### Key Narrative Arcs

1. **The Restoration (turns 300-328):** Northern Sapphire Coast is restored at turn 302 — the first restoration event in Aetheris. The restored state has a military-dominant faction (55%), breaking the merchant hegemony that had defined every government since the founding era. A power struggle erupts (turns 304-310), and the military faction wins. But the restored state cannot sustain itself — it is absorbed again at turn 328. This is the only civ in Aetheris to die, be reborn, and die again.

2. **Greater Western (turns 337-400):** Western NSC loses the Information Era and fractures — Greater Western Northern Sapphire Coast secedes, taking Thornwood. The naming has become self-parodying. Three states again divide the world, all with absurdly long names and absurdly small populations. Lower NSC suffers its own tech regression (industrial → renaissance). Knowledge is draining from the world.

3. **The Congress of Mistwood (turns 381-398):** Wars erupt between Greater Western NSC and Western NSC, with the same defender-wins pattern from the previous era. But at turn 398, something genuinely new occurs: the Congress of Mistwood achieves peace (importance 9). This is the first successful peace congress in Aetheris.
